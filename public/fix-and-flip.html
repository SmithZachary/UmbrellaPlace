Fix & Flip Loans

Short-term financing that covers the purchase and renovation of investment properties—so you can buy, renovate, and sell for a profit without tying up all your own cash.

What Is a Fix & Flip Loan?

A fix and flip loan is a short-term loan designed specifically for real estate investors who buy properties below market value, renovate them, and sell them for a profit. Unlike a traditional mortgage that might take 30-60 days to close, fix and flip loans are built for speed—often closing in as little as 7 to 14 days. The loan covers two things: the money to purchase the property and the money to pay for the renovations.

Here is the key thing to understand about the rehab portion: the renovation money is not handed to you all at once. Instead, the lender releases it in stages called draws. As you complete phases of the renovation—for example, demolition, rough plumbing, electrical, drywall, finishes—you request a draw, the lender sends an inspector to verify the work is done, and then releases the next portion of funds. This protects both you and the lender by making sure money is only spent on work that is actually completed.

The loan is repaid when you sell the renovated property (or refinance into a longer-term loan). Because the entire lifecycle is typically 6 to 18 months, you only pay interest for the time you use the money. There are no 30-year commitments and no long-term debt hanging over the project.

Who Is This For?

Fix and flip loans work for a wide range of real estate investors. If any of these sound like you, this loan type is worth exploring.

01

First-Time Flippers

You have found a property with potential and you are ready to do your first deal. Fix and flip loans let you get started without needing hundreds of thousands in cash on hand. Many lenders work with newer investors who bring a solid deal and a realistic plan.

02

Experienced Investors Scaling Up

You have done a few flips and you want to do more at the same time. Instead of waiting for one project to sell before starting the next, fix and flip financing lets you run multiple projects in parallel and grow your business faster.

03

Contractors Buying Their Own Projects

You already know how to do the work—now you want to keep the profit. If you are a licensed contractor or have deep renovation experience, lenders see that as a strength. You understand timelines, budgets, and construction quality better than most borrowers.

04

Investors Who Found a Deal but Need Fast Funding

Great deals do not wait. If you have a property under contract and need to close quickly—whether it is a foreclosure, estate sale, or off-market opportunity—fix and flip loans are designed for exactly this kind of speed. Close in days, not months.

Typical Terms

Every deal is different, but here is what fix and flip loan terms generally look like across our lending network.

$

Loan Amounts

$75K to $3M+ depending on the property and borrower experience. Larger deals are available for experienced investors with a proven track record.

%

Leverage (LTV)

Up to 90% of the purchase price and up to 100% of the rehab costs for experienced borrowers. First-time flippers can typically get 80-85% of purchase and 100% of rehab.

ARV

ARV-Based Lending

Most lenders cap total loan exposure at 70-75% of the After Repair Value (ARV). This is the projected market value of the property after renovations are complete—backed by comparable sales in the area.

%%

Interest Rates

Rates typically range from 9% to 13%, depending on borrower experience, credit score, property location, and deal strength. Lower rates are available for repeat borrowers with strong track records.

mo

Loan Terms

6 to 18 months. Most flips are completed in 4 to 8 months, but having a longer term gives you a cushion for unexpected delays. Extensions are often available if needed.

⚡

Speed to Close

Close in as little as 7 to 14 days. Because private lenders focus on the deal rather than months of income verification, the process moves fast. This is critical when competing for off-market deals.

What Makes a Good Flip?

Not every property is a good flip. Here are the factors that lenders (and experienced investors) look for when evaluating a deal.

01

Buy Price vs. ARV Spread

The most commonly referenced benchmark is the 70% rule: your total investment (purchase price plus rehab costs) should not exceed 70% of the After Repair Value. This leaves room for loan costs, holding costs, selling costs, and profit. The wider the spread, the safer the deal.

02

Realistic Rehab Budget

Experienced investors always include a 10-15% contingency on top of their rehab estimate. Surprises are the norm in renovation—hidden water damage, electrical issues, permitting delays. Get multiple contractor bids and budget conservatively.

03

Strong Comparable Sales

Your ARV estimate needs to be backed by recent comparable sales—ideally 3 or more properties within a mile that sold in the last 6 months. Lenders will verify this, and so should you. An inflated ARV is the fastest way to lose money on a flip.

04

Clear Timeline

A strong flip has a defined renovation schedule with realistic milestones. Know how long each phase will take, when you will list the property, and how long comparable homes are sitting on the market. Time is money—every extra month means more interest payments and holding costs.

05

Market Conditions

Look for markets with strong buyer demand, low inventory, and stable or rising home values. Avoid areas with declining populations, high days-on-market, or excessive new construction that could undercut your resale value. A great renovation in a weak market is still a bad deal.
