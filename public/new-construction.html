New Construction Loans

Ground-up financing for single-family, multi-family, and mixed-use builds.

What Is a Construction Loan?

A construction loan is a short-term loan designed to fund the building of a property from the ground up. Unlike a traditional mortgage where you receive the full loan amount at closing, a construction loan releases money in phases as the project progresses. These phases are called draws, and each one corresponds to a construction milestone—like pouring the foundation, completing the framing, or finishing the roof.

This phased structure protects both sides. You only pay interest on the money that has actually been disbursed, and the lender can verify that the project is on track before releasing the next round of funds. Think of it as a built-in system of checks and balances that keeps the project moving forward responsibly.

Private construction loans work differently from what you might get at a bank. Bank construction loans can take 60 to 90 days (or longer) to approve, require extensive personal financial documentation, and come with rigid requirements that can delay your project. Private construction loans, on the other hand, focus primarily on the deal itself—the property, the plans, and the builder’s track record. They can close in as little as two to three weeks, giving you the speed and flexibility to start building on your timeline, not the bank’s.

Who Is This For?

Construction loans serve a wide range of builders and investors. Here are some of the most common scenarios.

01

Developers Building Spec Homes

You’ve identified a market where new homes sell quickly. You need capital to build one or more homes on spec (without a buyer lined up yet), and you plan to sell them once complete. Private construction loans let you move fast and build on your own schedule.

02

Investors Building to Rent

If your strategy is to build new rental properties—whether single-family homes, duplexes, or small multi-family buildings—a construction loan covers the build phase. Once the property is finished and leased, you can refinance into a long-term rental loan (like a DSCR loan).

03

Builders Who Need Flexible Draw Schedules

Not every project follows a cookie-cutter timeline. If you need a draw schedule that aligns with your specific build plan—whether it’s a custom home or a phased development—private lenders can tailor the schedule to match your milestones.

04

Projects That Don’t Fit Bank Criteria

Banks often decline construction deals that involve raw land, entitled lots, unique property types, or borrowers with non-traditional income. Private construction loans are designed for exactly these situations—deals that make financial sense but don’t check every box on a bank’s checklist.

Typical Terms

Construction loan terms vary by project, but here is a general overview of what to expect from our lending network.

AMOUNT

$150K – $10M+

Loan amounts scale with the project. Whether you’re building a single home or a multi-unit development, our lenders can structure financing to match the scope of your build.

LTC

Up to 85%

Loan-to-Cost (LTC) measures how much of your total project cost the lender will finance. Up to 85% means you bring at least 15% to the table—covering your equity stake in the deal.

LTV

70 – 75% of Completed Value

Loan-to-Value (LTV) is based on what the property will be worth once it’s finished. Lenders typically cap this at 70–75%, ensuring there’s a healthy equity cushion in the completed project.

RATES

10 – 14%

Interest rates on private construction loans reflect the short-term, higher-risk nature of building a property. You only pay interest on the amount that has been drawn, not the full loan balance.

TERM

12 – 24 Months

Construction loans are short-term by design. The term covers the build period plus a buffer for selling or refinancing. Extensions are often available if needed.

CLOSING

10 – 21 Days

Private construction loans close significantly faster than bank loans. Once your plans, permits, and budget are in order, funding can happen in as little as 10 days.

The Construction Draw Process

If you’ve never built before, the draw process can seem unfamiliar. Here is how it works, step by step.

STEP 1

Initial Draw at Closing

When the loan closes, you receive your first draw. This typically covers the land acquisition (if included in the loan) and initial site work—things like grading, permits, and utility connections. This gets your project off the ground.

STEP 2

Milestone Inspections

As you hit construction milestones—foundation poured, framing complete, roof on, mechanical systems roughed in—an inspector visits the site to verify the work has been done. This is standard practice and usually takes just a day or two.

STEP 3

Draw Requests Submitted

Once a milestone passes inspection, you (or your general contractor) submit a draw request to the lender. This is a straightforward form that documents what work was completed, what it cost, and how much you’re requesting.

STEP 4

Funds Released

After the lender reviews and approves the draw request, funds are wired—typically within a few business days. This money covers the completed phase and allows you to pay contractors and suppliers, keeping the project moving forward.

STEP 5

Final Draw at Completion

The last draw is released when the project is finished and has passed its final inspection. At this point, the property is complete and you can move to your exit strategy—whether that’s selling the property, renting it out, or refinancing into a long-term loan.
