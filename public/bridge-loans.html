Bridge Loans

Short-term financing that helps you move fast on real estate deals—without waiting on banks or missing your window.

What Is a Bridge Loan?

A bridge loan is a short-term loan designed to “bridge the gap” between two events. In real estate, that usually means the gap between buying a property and either selling it, renovating and refinancing it, or securing long-term financing. Think of it as a temporary solution that gives you the capital to act now while you work toward a more permanent arrangement.

Here is a simple example: you find a great investment property, but your money is tied up in another deal that has not closed yet. A bridge loan gives you the funds to purchase the new property today, and you pay it back once your other deal closes or you secure a conventional loan. The property itself serves as collateral for the loan.

Bridge loans are funded by private lenders rather than traditional banks, which is why they can close in days instead of weeks or months. The trade-off is that interest rates are higher than a 30-year mortgage—but for investors who need speed and flexibility, the cost is well worth it. You are paying for certainty and timing, not just capital.

Who Is This For?

Bridge loans are built for borrowers who need speed, flexibility, or both. Here are the most common scenarios.

01

Investors Who Need to Close Fast

When a deal is time-sensitive—like a foreclosure auction, an off-market opportunity, or a competitive multi-offer situation—waiting 30 to 60 days for bank approval is not an option. A bridge loan lets you close in as little as 7 days so you do not lose the deal.

02

Borrowers Between Transactions

If your capital is locked in a property you are selling or refinancing, a bridge loan gives you immediate access to funds so you can move on your next acquisition without waiting. Once the first transaction completes, you pay off the bridge.

03

Properties That Don’t Qualify for Bank Financing

Banks have strict criteria around property condition, occupancy, and documentation. If your property needs work, is vacant, or falls outside a bank’s guidelines, a bridge loan from a private lender focuses on the asset value and the deal itself—not checkboxes.

04

Time-Sensitive Opportunities

Whether it is a discounted bulk portfolio, a motivated seller, or a deal that requires proof of funds within days, bridge loans give you the speed and certainty to act decisively when the window is narrow.
