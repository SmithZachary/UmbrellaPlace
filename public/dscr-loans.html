DSCR Rental Loans

Long-term financing based on your property's income, not yours.

What Is a DSCR Loan?

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It sounds technical, but it is actually a simple idea: can the rent from this property cover the mortgage payment? That is all lenders are looking at. If the answer is yes, you can qualify for the loan—regardless of your personal income, your W-2s, or your tax returns.

Here is a quick example. Say a rental property brings in $2,000 per month, and the full mortgage payment (including taxes, insurance, and any HOA dues) would be $1,600 per month. Divide the rent by the payment: $2,000 ÷ $1,600 = 1.25 DSCR. That means the property generates 25% more income than needed to cover the debt. Most lenders want to see a DSCR of 1.0 or higher, and 1.25 is considered strong.

The key benefit is that qualification is based entirely on the property's cash flow, not your personal finances. There are no W-2s to provide, no tax returns to explain, and no debt-to-income ratio to worry about. This makes DSCR loans ideal for self-employed investors, borrowers with complex tax situations, or anyone building a rental portfolio who has already maxed out conventional financing.

Who Is This For?

DSCR loans are designed for investors who want long-term rental financing without the hassle of traditional income documentation.

01

Buy-and-Hold Rental Investors

If you are purchasing a property to rent out long-term—whether it is a single-family home, a duplex, or a small apartment building—a DSCR loan lets you finance the deal based on what it earns, not what you earn.

02

Self-Employed Borrowers

Self-employed investors often show lower income on tax returns due to write-offs and deductions. DSCR loans sidestep that problem entirely. No tax returns, no profit-and-loss statements, no headaches proving income.

03

Portfolio Investors Scaling Up

Once you own five or more financed properties, conventional lenders start turning you away. DSCR loans have no limit on how many properties you can finance, making them the go-to option for investors building a larger portfolio.

04

Refinancing Out of Hard Money

If you used a short-term bridge or hard money loan to acquire and renovate a property, a DSCR loan is often the best way to refinance into long-term, stable debt once the property is rented and cash-flowing.

Understanding DSCR

The formula is straightforward. Here is exactly how lenders calculate it and what the numbers mean.

The Formula

DSCR = Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly Payment (PITIA)

What Is PITIA?

PITIA is the total monthly housing payment the lender uses in the DSCR calculation. It includes five components:

  • Principal — the portion of the payment that pays down the loan balance
  • Interest — the cost of borrowing the money
  • Taxes — annual property taxes divided by 12
  • Insurance — property insurance (hazard/homeowners) divided by 12
  • Association dues — HOA or condo fees, if applicable

Lenders add all of these together to get the full monthly obligation, then compare it to the property's rental income.

What the Numbers Mean

1.25

Strong

The property earns 25% more than the mortgage costs. This is a healthy cushion that most lenders are comfortable with.

1.0

Break-Even

Rent exactly covers the payment. Many lenders accept this, though rates may be slightly higher than a 1.25.

< 1.0

Below Break-Even

The rent does not fully cover the payment. Harder to qualify, but some programs go as low as 0.75 with a larger down payment.
